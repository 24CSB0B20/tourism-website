Qal'at al-Bahrain, also known as the Bahrain Fort, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Bahrain's most significant historical landmarks. This ancient site dates back to the Dilmun civilization, which flourished between 2300 BCE and 1600 BCE, though evidence of occupation stretches back even further. The fort itself was constructed by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later expanded by the Bahrainis. Strategically located on a hilltop, it provided defense against invaders while also overseeing trade routes across the Persian Gulf. The archaeological site includes the fort, as well as surrounding areas, revealing remnants of an ancient city, a defensive wall, wells, and houses. Excavations at the site have uncovered artifacts that shed light on Bahrain's role in ancient trade networks, including pottery, coins, and inscriptions. Today, Qal'at al-Bahrain is a must-visit site for history enthusiasts and those interested in Bahrain’s ancient civilization, offering a glimpse into the island’s fascinating past.
